TUM AR

Conceptual design for a map-based storytelling mobile app using augmented reality



Project Overview

Project type
Individual Research - M.Sc. Thesis

Deliverables
Mobile app mockup designs

Duration
6 months (Oct 2020 - Mar 2021)




Responsibilities
- Comparative analysis
- Conceptual design: mockups and wireframes
- Usabilities testing
- Analysis and formulating design recommendations

Tools used
Adobe Illustrator, Marvel


Problem statement

Maps are powerful tools for storytelling, yet modern advancements in mobile and web mapping often prioritize technical capabilities over intuitive and engaging user experiences. This gap in design focus inspired me to explore how emerging technologies like Augmented Reality (AR) could transform storytelling and navigation on mobile devices. Through a case study, I aimed to develop user-centered design recommendations that enhance usability and engagement in place-based AR experiences.


Challenges

My role was to design initial recommendations for a mobile AR app concept that guided users through a place of interest, blending storytelling with route visualization. One of the key challenges was to determine how multimedia elements could reduce visual clutter on smaller displays, making the interface more accessible and engaging. This led to the central question:
How can Augmented Reality (AR) transform map-based storytelling and navigation into an immersive, user-friendly experience?


Methodology

Case study area
I chose the TUM campus as the case study area, providing a focused and achievable scope to explore these ideas within the constraints of time and resources.
Target audience
The intended users were tourists visiting the historic 150-year-old TUM campus, as well as students—both new and returning—seeking an engaging and informative experience while navigating the campus.

Workflow
I employed a mixed-methods approach, combining comparative analysis, conceptual design, and usability testing.




Comparative analysis


To inform the conceptual design, I conducted a comparative study analyzing how map-based stories are visualized across three mediums: print, web, and AR/VR. I collected sample for each medium to identify patterns in using multimedia for designing narratives and navigation.





Key insights & pain points:

  • Print maps excel in aesthetics and narratives, but lack interactivity and connection to real-life places.
  • Web maps and AR apps often overload users with excessive dynamic elements leading to visual clutter especially on small screens.
  • Users often find it difficult to interact with AR/VR elements due to inconsistent sizing and placement, or lack of clear affordances.
  • Many AR/VR apps lack the combination of storytelling elements (e.g., narratives, points of interest) and navigation tools. Even when integrated, they are often siloed. This forces users to switch between views or even apps.




    • Conceptual design


    From the comparative analysis, I identified a detailed list of visual and multimedia elements used in the selected samples to indicate points of interest, convey narratives, visualize routes, and communicate additional information. Building on this, I developed a conceptual design that incorporates key elements tailored to the unique capabilities of AR while addressing challenges such as visual clutter and accessibility.










    Sketching & Wireframing


    I began the design process by creating initial sketches exploring how features like multimedia storytelling and route visualization could coexist in a user-friendly interface within the AR environment.


    To refine these ideas, I developed wireframes that prioritized:

  • Clear layout for integrating storytelling with navigation
  • Minimalistic use of visual elements to avoid clutter
  • Seamless transitions between 2D maps and AR view







    • Data collection and design process


    I gathered and prepared the visuals and multimedia elements needed for the mockup and narrative design including - a TUM campus map, text for story content, photographs of POIs, audio, video, and icons, buttons, and markers designed in Adobe Illustrator.

    I created GIFs to stimulate the immersive AR experience through the mockups using Gifmaker.me with real-life photos.

    The color palette for the main interface was chosen to reflect TU Munich’s branding, reinforcing familiarity, simplicity and ease of navigation. Other colors were assigned to different filter categories based on themes or real-life cues, such as dark blue is used for names of buildings and lecture halls at TUM, green indicates open spaces and so on.



    Finally, all processed elements were integrated into Marvel App to develop interactive mockups.









    Mockup design


    I developed high-fidelity mockups to translate conceptual ideas into a tangible design. The designs were made using Illustrator and then transformed into interactive mockups using Marvel.






    Usability testing

    For usability testing, I conducted semi-structured interviews with users, including TUM students and non-students (tourists). Participants were asked to perform tasks like navigate through the interactive mockups to reach a specific point of interest while thinking aloud to capture real-time reactions.

    Insights and User Feedback:
    Users responded positively to the idea of an AR tour but highlighted a need for more intuitive icons and clearer navigation markers. Shorter length of text is preferrable. Technical limitations in the mockup restricted interactivity, but feedback suggested that story pop-ups and multimedia elements would make it more engaging and combining route visualization in AR and map would be useful for navigation. Some elements were identified that invoked emotional responses from the users - color palette (students could relate with university branding), photos (curiosity & nostalgia), maps and route (feeling safe), narrative (engaging) and fonts (provocative).
